# 326: They lied to us about AI

> **Creator**: Mo Bitar | **Platform**: YouTube | **Date**: 2026-03-19 | **Duration**: 7:16

## Summary

Mo Bitar delivers a sharp, comedic critique of the AI industry's gap between marketing claims and operational reality. The video opens with OpenAI's internal "code red" -- new executive Fiji Simo declaring an organizational crisis not because AI became sentient, but because OpenAI has too many unfocused projects. Bitar's central irony: the company selling AI-powered productivity got less productive and solved the problem not with AI but with meetings, a PowerPoint, and a management hire from Facebook.

Bitar then contrasts OpenAI's product sprawl (Sora, Atlas browser, shopping feature, rumored airline) with Anthropic's disciplined focus on just two things: coding tools and enterprise. He pivots to the deeper argument: AI is not replacing developers, citing the METR randomized control study showing senior developers were 19% slower with AI assistance. The 41% of code now written by AI is largely going "into the trash" -- creating review burden rather than productivity gains. His sharpest claim: the AI that could replace jobs (AGI) is imaginary, while the AI that exists (LLMs) cannot replace jobs because it lacks the ability to learn on the job or handle company-specific, undocumented, judgment-heavy work.

## Key Concepts

### The Productivity Paradox

OpenAI's internal crisis exemplifies the AI productivity paradox: the company building productivity tools cannot organize itself productively. Bitar frames this as a fundamental credibility problem -- if AI truly delivered the productivity gains being marketed, OpenAI would be the most productive company in the world, not one drowning in unfocused projects needing a management consultant from Facebook.

### Focus as Competitive Advantage

Anthropic's success against OpenAI is attributed to disciplined focus: coding tools and enterprise, nothing else. No browser, no TikTok clone, no image generation. Claude Code's Christmas "bender" (developers binge-using it over the holiday) is cited as evidence that focused execution on the right problems beats scattered product launches.

### The METR Study and the 19% Slowdown

A randomized control study by METR recruited 16 senior developers working on real codebases with real tasks and found AI usage made them 19% slower. Bitar uses this to argue that the 41% of code now AI-generated is creating net negative productivity -- it floods codebases with code that senior developers must review, debug, and often discard.

### The LLM-AGI Bait and Switch

Bitar articulates the rhetorical sleight of hand at the industry's core: companies released LLMs, marketed them as "AI," then when limitations became apparent, shifted the narrative to "the real AI is AGI, coming soon." He frames this as a classic "my girlfriend goes to a different school" deflection. LLMs predict the next word based on internet patterns; they cannot learn on the job. The work that matters in every company is "super specific, super custom stuff that requires deep judgment and company context that doesn't exist anywhere on the internet."

## Practical Takeaways

- **AI creates a second job, not a replacement**: The current reality is that AI-generated code creates review and cleanup burden for senior engineers rather than eliminating engineering roles.
- **Focus beats feature sprawl**: Anthropic's success with fewer people, less money, and less compute is a lesson in product discipline.
- **Distinguish between LLM capabilities and AGI promises**: The technology that exists (pattern matching, next-token prediction) is fundamentally different from the technology being promised (general intelligence), and the industry benefits from conflating them.
- **Company-specific work is the moat**: The work AI cannot do is the undocumented, judgment-heavy, context-specific work that defines every organization's actual operations.
