## Engagement Patterns (Early Observations)

### What works
- **Transfix-specific framing**: The "Transfix Brain" post (1,075 impressions, 28 reactions) massively outperformed the abstract "company brain concept" post (268 impressions, 6 reactions) despite covering similar ground. "Practice what you preach" with a named product beats abstract thought leadership.
- **Product announcement energy**: Posts that feel like news ("We are calling it...") drive more reach than posts that feel like commentary ("There's a concept trending...").
- **Concrete names and numbers**: Naming tools (Claude Code, MCP), citing specific sources (280+ sources), and referencing real team structure drives credibility.

### What underperforms
- **Abstract/analytical framing**: The company brain concept post had the most thoughtful analysis but lowest reach. LinkedIn rewards specificity and story over analysis.
- **No Transfix connection**: Posts without a direct Transfix tie-in get roughly 25-50% of the impressions of those with one.

### Audience profile
- Consistently **Senior seniority** (33-50%)
- Primarily **NYC Metro** (31-44%)
- Mix of **Software Development** and **Transportation/Logistics** industries
- **Software Engineer** is the top job title (12-20%)
- **Transfix employees** represent 5-14% of viewers

### Metrics to watch
- Impressions-to-engagement ratio (currently ~3% for best post)
- Profile viewers per post (proxy for thought leadership impact)
- Follower growth per post
- Save count (proxy for reference value)
