### Specification-First Development Becomes Consensus

Matt Pocock's seven-phase framework (#221) makes explicit what practitioners have been converging on: prototyping before PRDs, specifications before execution, human QA as the final gate. IBM Technology (#214) formally names "spec-driven development." Jaymin West (#220) adds enforcement — hooks, quality gates, and a "never fix bad output" philosophy. The discourse has shifted from "should I vibe code?" to "how do I write specifications agents can execute reliably?"

**Sources**: [#221](../sources/221-matt-pocock-seven-phases-ai-development.md), [#214](../sources/214-ibm-technology-spec-driven-development.md), [#220](../sources/220-jaymin-west-anti-slop-engineering.md)

### Jeremy Howard's Devastating AI Coding Critique

Deep learning pioneer Jeremy Howard (#226) distinguishes coding (translating specs to syntax — LLMs do well) from software engineering (designing abstractions for novel problems — LLMs cannot). His gambling addiction analogy for AI coding workflows — stochastic rewards, illusion of control, losses disguised as wins — provides a psychological framework for the gap between reported and measured productivity gains. Harvard (#215) extends this to education: AI shortcuts erode understanding under novel conditions.

**Sources**: [#226](../sources/226-mlst-ai-coding-illusion.md), [#215](../sources/215-harvard-ai-learning-education.md), [#217](../sources/217-primetime-bullshit-benchmark.md)
