### Specification-First Development Becomes Consensus

Matt Pocock's seven-phase framework (#145) makes explicit what practitioners have been converging on: prototyping before PRDs, specifications before execution, human QA as the final gate. IBM Technology (#138) formally names "spec-driven development." Jaymin West (#144) adds enforcement — hooks, quality gates, and a "never fix bad output" philosophy. The discourse has shifted from "should I vibe code?" to "how do I write specifications agents can execute reliably?"

### Jeremy Howard's Devastating AI Coding Critique

Deep learning pioneer Jeremy Howard (#150) distinguishes coding (translating specs to syntax — LLMs do well) from software engineering (designing abstractions for novel problems — LLMs cannot). His gambling addiction analogy for AI coding workflows — stochastic rewards, illusion of control, losses disguised as wins — provides a psychological framework for the gap between reported and measured productivity gains. Harvard (#139) extends this to education: AI shortcuts erode understanding under novel conditions.

**Sources**: [#150](../sources/150-mlst-ai-coding-illusion.md), [#139](../sources/139-harvard-ai-learning-education.md), [#141](../sources/141-primetime-bullshit-benchmark.md)
